Putin critic Navalny gets reprieve as court finds fault with case Opposition leader Alexei Navalny talks to the media after leaving a justice court building in Moscow April 22 2014. MOSCOW (Reuters) - A Moscow court on Monday returned a theft case against opposition leader Alexei Navalny to prosecutors on Monday Russian news agencies reported handing a reprieve to the one of President Vladimir Putin's biggest critics. The ruling means the second trial of the protest leader who is already serving a five-year suspended sentence will be put off at least until prosecutors remove flaws in their case.
